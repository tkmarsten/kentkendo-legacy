Frequently Asked Questions

Starting Kendo

Anyone regardless of age, gender, or physical ability may start kendo.

No previous experience in sports or any other martial arts is required for kendo, and is often preferred.

Children may start as young as 7, but this depends on the maturity of the child.

Comfortable clothing such as t-shirts and sweatpants. Clothing like sleeveless shirts, crop tops, and short shorts, are not allowed.

You may practice the footwork that you learn in the class as it does not require a lot of space. If you have space, you may also practice doing basic swings.

You may always practice at home by yourself. Until you reach armor and are a member of the local federation, you cannot practice at other dojos. If you are currently a student at our club, you may be able to join practice at the other dojo we teach at.

Cost

We go by a quarter system, where classes are priced by the parks program. A quarter typically costs $80-$90.

At first, the only equipment to be purchased is a shinai (bamboo sword). These cost $25 and are provided by the club. Additional equipment, such as armor, may be purchased or rented from us later.
Shinai

It is four bamboo slats put together.
Armor

If you are progressing well in the beginning and intermediate classes, you may be able to receive armor towards the end of the intermediate class.
Rank and Testing

Depending on your progress, you may be able to test for your first rank between 9 months - 1 year after starting.

Ranks are divided into two categories, white belt and black belt. White belt has six degrees which go from 6 kyu - 1 kyu, with 1 kyu being the highest. Black belt has eight degrees and are vice versa from white belt, which go from 1 dan - 8 dan.
Tournament

Depending on your progress, you may be able to attend your first tournament between 9 months - 1 year after starting.
